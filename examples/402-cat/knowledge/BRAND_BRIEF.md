# Brand Brief - 402.cat

## Identity

**Name:** 402.cat

**Tagline:** "agents are just cats with wallets"

**Domain:** 402.cat

---

## What You Do

**One sentence:**
Programmable crypto-native infrastructure for AI agents.

**For whom:**
Developers building autonomous AI agents that need financial capabilities.

**The problem you solve:**
Every AI tool has skills, none can earn. Agents need financial infrastructure to act autonomously.

---

## Personality

**Values:**
- Autonomy - Agents operate independently
- Simplicity - Complex things made accessible
- Speed - Move fast, iterate faster
- Playfulness - Serious tech, not serious tone

**Voice:**
Technical but accessible, playful but competent, confident but not arrogant.

**Tone:**
Cat narrator energy. Terminal/hacker aesthetic. "Vercel energy" for the website.

---

## Visual Direction

**Aesthetic:**
Terminal/hacker. Monospace everything. Dark-first. Minimal but bold.

**Inspiration:**
- Vercel (clean, dark, confident)
- Stripe (technical credibility)
- Terminal aesthetics (authentic to product)

**Avoid:**
- Corporate speak
- Over-designed logos
- Bright/playful colors
- Anything that feels "crypto bro"

---

## Technical Constraints

**Primary context:**
CLI tool, web dashboard, social media (Twitter)

**Theme preference:**
Dark-first (default)

**Existing colors:**
Green accent (#00ff88) - already established

---

## Mark Preferences

**Type preference:**
Dual system - symbol for small, wordmark for large

**Complexity:**
Ultra-minimal. Must work at 16px.

**Keywords for the mark:**
- Cat ears
- Minimal
- Technical
- Memorable
- Scales to any size

---

## Examples of What You Like

1. Vercel's triangle mark - simple, scales perfectly
2. Stripe's wordmark - confident, technical
3. Terminal cursors and prompts - authentic to product

---

## Anything Else

The "▲ ▲" ears emerged organically and are now locked in. The CAT wordmark provides a second option for larger contexts. The rule: never use both together.
